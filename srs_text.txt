Introduction
1.1
Purpose
This document specifies the software requirements for the Meeting and Communication
Management platform. The system aims to reduce meeting friction and improve outcome
traceability by supporting the complete meeting lifecycle including scheduling, structured
agenda preparation, in-meeting documentation, automated transcript processing, action-
item tracking, and searchable archives.
The platform emphasizes agenda-driven meetings, integrated calendar coordination,
AI-assisted summarization and action extraction, and structured follow-ups to ensure
that meetings translate into measurable outcomes rather than isolated discussions.
1.2
Document Conventions
This document follows the IEEE 830-1998 standard. Requirements are prioritized using
the MoSCoW method (Must, Should, Could, Won’t) and (Vital, Essential and Desirable).
Each functional requirement is uniquely identified as REQ-x.
1.3
Intended Audience and Reading Suggestions
This document is intended for project developers, university instructors, and AI/ML
engineers. Focus on Section 2.1 for the Agenda Workflow and Section 3 for the technical
requirements of the host-driven recording.
1.4
Project Scope
The Meeting and Communication Management platform provides a centralized system
for managing structured, agenda-driven meetings and their associated outcomes. The
system bridges the gap between video conferencing tools and task management systems
by integrating scheduling, live meeting support, transcript processing, and action tracking
within a unified workflow.
During meetings, a host-controlled agenda structure guides discussions and enables
contextual segmentation of captured transcripts.
Post-meeting, the system processes
recorded content to generate structured summaries and automatically identify action
items linked to specific agenda topics.
By combining structured execution with automated follow-ups and searchable archives,
the platform ensures that meetings are organized, traceable, and outcome-oriented rather
than isolated discussions.

Overall Description
2.1
Product Perspective
The Meeting and Communication Management System (MCMS) functions as an intelli-
gence layer built on top of existing communication and calendar infrastructures. Rather
than replacing video conferencing tools, the system augments them by orchestrating a
structured, agenda-driven workflow that connects meeting preparation, execution, and
follow-up management.
The architecture of the system is organized into three primary functional domains:
2.1.1
Pre-Meeting Agenda Orchestration:
Prior to a meeting, the host defines a structured agenda (or can be automated) consisting
of ordered discussion topics, time allocations, and associated reference materials. This
agenda serves as both the operational roadmap for the live session and the structural
framework for post-meeting documentation and summarization.
2.1.2
Synchronous Interaction & Agenda Tracking:
During the live session, the system integrates with a video conferencing provider to enable
real-time collaboration.
A synchronized agenda interface guides participants through
discussion topics and maintains alignment with the predefined structure.
The system captures meeting content for subsequent transcription and processing.
The captured data is temporally aligned with agenda segments to preserve contextual
relevance.
2.1.3
Post-Meeting Intelligence:
Following the meeting, the system processes the transcript to generate structured sum-
maries and identify decisions, action items, and off-topic discussions (“parking lot” items).
By mapping transcript segments to agenda topics, the system enables topic-specific doc-
umentation, outcome traceability, and searchable knowledge archival.
2.2
User Classes and Characteristics
The Meeting and Communication Management System (MCMS) serves users across mul-
tiple institutional environments and meeting contexts. Users are classified based on sys-
tem roles, institutional affiliation, and meeting usage scenarios. These classifications help
define access permissions, interaction patterns, and functional expectations.
2.2.1
Role-based User Classes
• Host/Owner: Initiates and manages meetings, advances agenda items, and initi-
ates audio capture.
• Administrator: Manages user access, configures integrations, monitors API usage
for Sarvam AI and maintains organizational settings.
6
• Participant: Views live agenda progress, contributes to live discussions and re-
ceives assigned tasks and has access to post-meeting summaries.
• Observer: Has only read-only access to meeting outputs like minutes, search
archives and monitors decisions without modification privileges.
2.2.2
Institutional User Classification
• Academic Users: Faculty and students participating in instructional or evaluative
meetings, requires structured agendas and participation tracking.
• Administrative Users: Institutional staff managing organizational discussions
and follow-ups, emphasis on action tracking and accountability.
• Institutional Authorities:
Organizational administrators overseeing multiple
meetings and archives
2.2.3
Meeting Context-Based Usage
• Classroom Meetings:
One-host (instructor) with multiple participants (stu-
dents).
Agenda-driven sessions and focus on structured delivery and documen-
tation.
• Viva/Interview Sessions: Few evaluators with controlled participation. Staged
entry mechanisms and emphasis on recording and decision documentation.
• Administrative Meetings: Decision-oriented discussions with heavy use of agen-
das and action-item tracking. Post-meeting follow-up is critical.
2.3
Contextual Use-Case Scenarios
This subsection outlines representative real-world meeting contexts supported by the
system. These scenarios help clarify operational expectations, trigger conditions, and
environmental constraints across academic, professional, and informal settings.
2.3.1
Online Class in Academic Settings
• Primary Actor: Teacher
• Goal in Context: To deliver structured educational content and facilitate inter-
action with students in a controlled online environment.
• Pre-conditions:
– Stable internet connectivity for both instructor and students.
– Participants must join using identifiable and institutionally valid names.
• Trigger: The scheduled class start time as defined by the academic timetable or
calendar synchronization.
7
2.3.2
Interview During Hiring Process
• Primary Actors: Interviewer and Interviewee
• Goal in Context: To evaluate the candidate’s technical skills, communication
ability, and professional conduct.
• Pre-conditions:
– Candidate is expected to be in formal attire.
– Proper lighting and a distraction-free environment on the candidate’s side.
– Camera must remain enabled throughout the interview to maintain integrity
and prevent malpractice.
• Trigger: Interviewer initiates the meeting at the scheduled interview time.
2.3.3
Informal Family Meetings
• Primary Actor: All Participants
• Goal in Context: To promote social interaction and visual engagement among
all members of the family.
• Pre-conditions:
– Video streams of all participants should be visible simultaneously, especially
for meetings involving 10–15 members, to enhance inclusivity and interaction.
• Trigger: Any participant initiating a family call or scheduled family gathering
session.
2.3.4
Strategy Discussion and Quantitative Planning
• Primary Actor: All Participants
• Goal in Context: To collaboratively analyze quantitative data and resource allo-
cation after completing qualitative discussions.
• Pre-conditions:
– The meeting environment should provide relevant resources in agendas and
the analysis discussions should be manually or automatically summarized and
if any actions are assigned to any participants, they should be followed up.
• Trigger: Completion of qualitative analysis and initiation of quantitative evalua-
tion by any participant or the meeting host.
8
2.3.5
Friendly Board Games and Social Play
• Primary Actor: All Participants
• Goal in Context: To enable casual social interaction through multiplayer board
and word-based games during virtual meetups.
• Pre-conditions:
– No formal preconditions; participants may join freely.
– Optional integration of game engines (e.g., Chess or Go engines) and combi-
natorial calculators for enhanced gameplay experience.
• Trigger: Participants selecting a game-based interaction mode during or after
joining a meeting.
2.4
Product Features
• Live Synchronized Agenda Panel with host-led navigation.
• Host-side Audio Capture and buffering for STT processing.
• Segmented AI Summaries (Minutes of Meeting categorized by topic).
• Real-time ”Parking Lot” for capturing off-topic discussions.
• Searchable Archive of past meetings mapped to agenda history.
2.5
Operating Environment
The system is a web application accessible via modern browsers.
The backend uses
Node.js for WebSocket-based agenda synchronization and a Python service for AI pro-
cessing.
2.6
Design and Implementation Constraints
2.6.1
Hardware and Browser Security Constraints
Audio capture is strictly constrained by modern browser security models; the Host must
provide explicit manual permission for system audio access via the MediaDevices API.
The system cannot programmatically bypass the user-prompt for ”Share System Audio,”
which is a mandatory requirement for capturing multi-party dialogue.
2.6.2
Component Synchronization
The system must maintain sub-second synchronization between the external Jitsi IFrame
and the custom React-based Agenda Panel. Failure to maintain state-synchronization
may result in misaligned time-stamps for agenda-item transitions and note-taking.
9
2.6.3
AI Diarization and Attribution Limitations
Accurate speaker identification (Diarization) presents a significant technical constraint.
Since the audio is captured as a single merged stream from the host’s system output,
the AI engine (Sarvam AI) must rely on acoustic fingerprinting to distinguish between
speakers. The system may face reduced accuracy in ”Entity Attribution” (mapping names
to dialogue) in environments with high background noise or overlapping speech.
2.6.4
NLU Entity Extraction Accuracy
The extraction of specific ”Action Items” and ”Deadlines” is subject to the limitations
of Named Entity Recognition (NER) models. Ambiguous natural language (e.g., ”let’s
handle this later”) may not reliably trigger automated task creation, requiring a manual
verification step by the meeting host to ensure outcome traceability.

2.8
Assumptions and Dependencies
• Network Stability: Assumes the Host maintains a stable, high-bandwidth con-
nection to facilitate uninterrupted audio streaming and batch file uploads.
• Vendor Availability: The system depends on the Sarvam AI platform for high-
accuracy transcription of Indian English and Hindi-mixed dialogue (code-switching).
• Speaker Identification Protocol: It is assumed that participants will verbally
state their names or address others by name during the initial phases of the meeting.
This behavioral protocol is essential to assist the NLU model in mapping acoustic
diarization segments to specific stakeholder identities.
• Hardware Permissions: Assumes that all users have functional audio hardware
and will grant the necessary browser-level permissions for system audio capture as
required by the security model.

System Features
3.1
Meeting Initialization and Scheduling (Vital)
3.1.1
Description and Priority
This feature allows the host to coordinate participant availability through a democratic
polling process and formalizes the meeting environment (Online, Offline, or Hybrid). It
ensures the meeting is synchronized with external calendars once a consensus on timing
is reached.
3.1.2
Stimulus/Response Sequences
• Host initiates meeting: Host enters meeting details and selects multiple potential
dates/times →System generates a scheduling poll.
• Participants vote: Participants select preferred slots →System aggregates re-
sults.
• Finalization: Host (or system auto-logic) confirms the winning slot →System
sends calendar invites (Google/Outlook) and provisionally locks the meeting modal-
ity resources.
3.1.3
Functional Requirements
• REQ-1: The system shall allow hosts to propose at least five (5) distinct time slots
for participant voting.
• REQ-2: The system shall support three meeting modalities:
11
– Online: Automatic generation of a Jitsi meeting URL.
– Offline: Requirement of a physical ”Place/Room” attribute and local map
data.
– Hybrid: Simultaneous provisioning of a virtual link and a physical location.
• REQ-3: Upon poll completion, the system shall utilize two-way calendar synchro-
nization to inject the event into the host’s and participants’ calendars.
3.2
In-Meeting Attendance and Polling System (QR-Based)(Essential)
3.2.1
Description and Priority
High priority (Must). This subsection governs the verification of physical/virtual presence
and enables rapid sentiment collection during the live session using mobile-accessible QR
codes.
3.2.2
Stimulus/Response Sequences
• Attendance Check: Host triggers ”Attendance QR” →System displays a unique,
time-sensitive QR code on the shared screen →Participants scan via mobile →
System logs verified attendance in the meeting metadata.
• Live Polling: Host launches a mid-meeting poll →System generates a QR code
→Participants scan to access the voting interface →Live results are visualized in
the Agenda Panel.
3.2.3
Functional Requirements
• REQ-1: The system shall generate dynamic, non-reusable QR codes for attendance
tracking to prevent fraudulent check-ins.
• REQ-2: The system shall provide an automated attendance report at the end of
the session, mapping scanned IDs to the original invitee list.
• REQ-3: The system shall support real-time ”Instant Polls” where the host can
input a question and generate a QR link for immediate participant feedback during
the live session.
3.3
Agenda Panel
3.3.1
Synchronized Agenda Panel (Vital)
Description and Priority
Serves as the real-time navigational anchor for the meeting,
ensuring all participants are aligned with the current topic and providing the temporal
structure for AI-based metadata tagging.
12
Functional Requirements
• REQ-SYNC-1: Real-time Item Tracking: The system shall visually highlight
the active agenda item in the user interface for all participants simultaneously.
• REQ-SYNC-2: Host Control & Navigation: The system shall provide the
Meeting Host with controls to ”Start,” ”Pause,” and ”Complete” agenda items,
triggering automated time-stamping in the background.
• REQ-SYNC-3: Dynamic Time Allocation: The system shall provide a visual
”countdown” or progress bar based on the predefined time estimates for each item
to surface meeting efficiency.
• REQ-SYNC-4: AI Metadata Anchoring: (Essential) The system shall au-
tomatically map the live audio transcript segments to the active agenda item ID to
enable structured searching in the meeting archive.
3.3.2
Asynchronous Agenda Panel (Essential)
Description and Priority
Facilitates pre-meeting preparation and post-meeting re-
view cycles, allowing stakeholders to interact with the meeting structure independently
of a live session.
Functional Requirements
• REQ-AS-1: Pre-meeting Contribution: The system shall allow authorized
participants to add agenda items, attach pre-read documents, and post clarifying
questions before the meeting commences.
• REQ-AS-2: Offline Metadata Caching: The panel shall utilize local browser
storage (IndexedDB/Service Workers) to allow users to view and edit agendas in
low-connectivity campus environments, synchronizing changes once a stable con-
nection is restored.
• REQ-AS-3: Post-Meeting Annotation: Following the meeting conclusion, the
panel shall transform into a ”Minutes Review” mode, allowing users to verify AI-
extracted action items against the recorded agenda segments.
• REQ-AS-4: Async Decision Logging: For hybrid or distributed teams, the
system shall support ”Asynchronous Voting” on agenda items to reach a consensus
without requiring a synchronous video call.
3.3.3
Stimulus/Response Sequences
Host clicks ”Next Item” →System updates UI for all participants →System logs times-
tamp for AI segmentation →Countdown timer resets.
3.3.4
Functional Requirements
• REQ-1: The system shall synchronize the active agenda item across all participant
screens via WebSockets.
• REQ-2: The system shall display a visual countdown timer for each agenda topic.
13
3.4
Host-Driven Audio Transcription (Vital)
3.4.1
Description and Priority
Captures audio from the host’s interface to feed the Sarvam AI engine.
3.4.2
Stimulus/Response Sequences
Host starts recording →Browser captures audio stream →Audio segments sent to back-
end →Live transcript appears in UI sidebar.
3.4.3
Functional Requirements
• REQ-3: The system shall capture host-side audio to ensure a consistent stream
for the AI pipeline.
• REQ-4: The system shall tag transcript segments with the Active Agenda ID for
contextual summarization.
3.5
Sentiment Analysis and Intelligence Extraction (Desirable)
Description and Priority
Utilizes Natural Language Processing (NLP) to evaluate
the emotional tone of the meeting and provides a fail-safe manual override for data
integrity.
Functional Requirements
• REQ-AI-1:
Sentiment Scoring: The system shall analyze the transcript to
assign a sentiment score (Positive, Neutral, Negative) to specific agenda segments.
• REQ-AI-2: Conflict Detection: The system shall flag segments with high neg-
ative sentiment or rapid speaker turnover as ”Potential Conflict Areas” for host
review.
• REQ-AI-3: Manual Override Route: The system shall provide a ”Manual

AI Virtual Participant and Real-Time Interaction (Desirable)
3.17.1
Meeting Integration Layer
The platform shall implement a specialized integration layer that allows a virtual AI
bot to join sessions as a participant. For the Jitsi-based implementation, this is achieved
through the Jitsi Meet External API and background media stream listeners. This layer is
responsible for establishing a stable connection to the meeting and providing the backend
with direct access to the real-time audio and chat streams.
20
3.17.2
Active and Passive Operational Modes
The system shall support two distinct modes of AI participation, switchable via the host
dashboard or in-meeting chat commands:
• Passive Mode: The bot acts as a silent observer, performing continuous audio
capture and background transcription without providing verbal or textual feedback
during the live session.
• Active Mode: The bot is empowered to interact with participants. In this mode,
the bot can answer contextual questions based on the meeting history, provide real-
time clarifications, and post live reminders of upcoming agenda items directly into
the meeting chat.
3.17.3
Transcription and Intelligence Pipeline
Once the integration layer secures the audio stream, the data is routed through a high-
performance pipeline:
• High-Accuracy Transcription: The system utilizes Sarvam AI to convert multi-
party conversations into text. This choice is optimized for low-latency processing
and high accuracy across Indian languages and regional accents.
• LLM-Driven Analysis: The resulting transcript is fed into a Large Language
Model (LLM) backend. This allows the platform to perform real-time topic detec-
tion, speaker-wise segmentation, and the extraction of key discussion highlights.
3.17.4
Post-Meeting Synthesis and Documentation
Following the conclusion of the session, the system automatically aggregates all processed
data to generate comprehensive documentation:
• Minutes of Meeting (MoM): Automated generation of structured summaries
including decisions made and contextual background.
• Action Item Mapping: Specific identification of tasks using Named Entity Recog-
nition (NER), mapping ”who” is responsible for ”what” by ”when.”
• Export and Sync: Finalized notes can be reviewed on the web dashboard and
exported to PDF/Word formats or synchronized with external productivity tools
such as Google Docs or Notion.
